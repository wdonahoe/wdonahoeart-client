As an artist, I work primarily in graphite, charcoal, and colored pencil on paper. I earned my bachelor of fine arts degree in 1978 from the University of Delaware, from which I also received the Trevania Robinson Art Prize... as a drawing major. Drawing has been a lifelong passion and remains my chosen vehicle for artistic expression.

I grew up in Newark, Delaware, in a planned community of cookie-cutter houses called "Brookside". As a child I began to draw from the Jones Family album of old sepia toned photographs as a means to get to know my father, who had died before my first birthday. Living in Newark afforded me the opportunity to attend the University of Delaware where I received top-notch instruction from many notable artists. Realist artist, Steve Tanis, introduced me to a "continuous tone" method of drawing which I have used ever since, and developed as my signature style.

In 1980 I moved to Northern Virginia to pursue a masters degree at George Washington University. Unfortunately the realities of the high cost of graduate school, and living on my own, would only allow for one year. While there however, I received invaluable instruction from Scip Barnhart, who at the time used stone lithography to create prints that appeared to be finely detailed pencil drawings. In a class taught by renowned artist, William Woodward, I learned how the Old Masters used transparent glazes in oil paintings, the concept which I've applied in the layering of colored pencil.

I decided to stay in the DC area, but as I struggled to make a living, it became necessary to relegate art as a sideline; it would remain as such until after my marriage and the birth of my children. I rejoined The Art League in the late 1990's and have regularly had my work selected by jury to monthly shows in the Torpedo Factory Art Center. Along with my husband Will, and children, Wallace and Avery, I reside in Alexandria, VA and in Gerrardstown, WV where we recently completed construction on an eco dwelling and studio www.broomgrass.com.

I was honored to be a selected artist for an exhibition that examined current trends in drawing, "Between The Lines: Contemporary Drawing Now". Held at the Torpedo Factory's Target Gallery, the juror, DC based artist, Linn Meyer, presented some very insightful thoughts on the subject. "Drawing has long been understood to be a preparatory medium. Drawing today, however, is a medium unto itself and must go well beyond the thin idea of a work made in preparation for artwork that is much larger or more important. A finished drawing must be fully realized both formally and conceptually. Artists are using drawing as a primary mode of expression in the 21st century".
